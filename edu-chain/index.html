All about Web3

What is web3?

“Web3 is the internet owned by the builders and users, orchestrated with tokens.” — Chris Dixon

Web3 represents a foundational evolution of the internet. Built on blockchain architectures, Web3 moves from centralized control and capitalization to a decentralized network in which capital is distributed between operators and consumers.

Web1
1990 to 2000 — Read-only

  • Invention of HTML and the World Wide Web.

  • The first commercial web browser created containing static websites of text and images.

  • Limited user interactivity and content-sharing capabilities.

Web 2.0
2000 to 2015 — Read-write

  • Blogs, wikis, and social media sites enable user-generated content, interactivity, and real-time collaboration.

  • Focus on web standards, accessibility, and usability.

  • Reliance on ads and user data drives business models.

Web3
2015 to NOW — Read-write-own

  • Decentralized networks offer users security, accountability, transparency, and control over their data.

  • Introduction to widescale blockchain technology, smart contracts, and distributed ledgers for one-way information sharing.

A team of web3 experts

BlockJoy started as a project to manage staking with friends and family.
